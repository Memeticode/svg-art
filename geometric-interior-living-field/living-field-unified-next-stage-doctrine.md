# Living Field — Unified Next-Stage Doctrine

This document is intended to be handed directly to Claude as the unified next-stage direction for the Living Field project.

The project has reached a point where isolated tweaks are no longer enough. The next meaningful leap will come from treating all the current ideas as parts of one coherent system rather than a collection of interesting enhancements.

The guiding idea is:

**The Living Field should become a climate-driven, unresolved geometric ecology where all visible structure is temporary, all major forces extend beyond the frame, gradients act as climate evidence, local forms inherit memory and contagion, and overlaps sometimes imply impossible spatial logic.**

That is the central sentence. Everything below should serve it.

---

## Core Design Law

The field is not a composition of objects.

The field is a **slice through a larger impossible climate**.

That means:

- major structures begin and end off-screen
- local motifs are temporary inhabitants of larger forces
- gradients are not decoration; they are evidence of climate exposure
- overlap is not layering for prettiness; it is a source of spatial contradiction
- the system remembers prior states
- neighboring structures infect one another
- regions have dialects
- eras shift slowly
- motifs do not resolve cleanly
- the viewer never gets a final stable reading

---

# The Major Subsystems to Combine

## 1. Dynamic Climate Field

Do not treat the field as a static flow map.

The climate should evolve on multiple timescales:

- **Deep field**: slow structural identity
- **Seasonal field**: long drift in regional character
- **Weather field**: medium-timescale local changes
- **Micro field**: subtle turbulence and pressure variation

This gives the system:

- memory
- drift
- era shifts
- dynamic boundaries
- local shocks
- more believable climate authorship

### Guidance
The field should evolve like weather, not jitter like noise.

Good behavior:
- attractors migrate slowly
- lanes thicken, thin, fork, or bend
- basins deepen and relax
- curl zones emerge, intensify, and decay
- regional dialect boundaries soften and reform

Bad behavior:
- abrupt re-randomization
- noisy frame-to-frame changes
- directional snapping
- climate forgetting itself

---

## 2. Off-Screen Continuity Doctrine

Major structures should not live comfortably inside the frame.

Apply this rule at multiple levels:

- macro climate traces should enter and exit off-screen
- many mid-scale structures should be partially clipped by the viewport
- some motifs can be fully visible, but most important large structures should not be

This creates:

- a world beyond the frame
- less “placed object” feeling
- more focus on local dynamics
- stronger climate reading
- greater scale and mystery

### Guidance
The viewport should feel like a cut through a larger field, not a self-contained canvas.

---

## 3. Climate-Driven Gradients

Gradients should be treated as **field readouts**, not styling.

Use gradients to reveal:

- pressure-facing side
- decay-facing side
- recent exposure history
- front direction
- basin depth
- curl influence
- residue age

### Strong guidance
- mostly use linear, directional, or path-aligned gradients
- use very few radial gradients
- favor asymmetrical gradients
- favor opacity gradients more than rainbow gradients
- use different gradient behavior for macro traces, motifs, and residue

### Why
Gradients should not beautify shapes.
They should show what has happened to them.

---

## 4. Identity Decay / Anti-Resolution

No meaningful motif should remain comfortable for long.

Important motifs should continually renegotiate themselves through:

- shell gap shifts
- lobe dominance changes
- scaffold imbalance
- contour redistribution
- puncture migration
- fragment shedding
- partial collapse
- asymmetry creep

The field should behave like:

**an equation that never resolves.**

### Rule
No important motif should remain readable as a stable symbol for long.

---

## 5. Memory and Residue

The field should remember.

Use:

- motif memory
- climate exposure memory
- shell collapse memory
- neighbor influence memory
- residue traces after reinterpretation
- macro scars from earlier weather

This adds:

- time
- consequence
- weathering
- the sense that the world existed before the current frame

### Guidance
Residue should be:
- restrained
- soft
- temporary
- strongest on major reinterpretation events

Too much residue becomes clutter.
Too little residue makes the field feel forgetful.

---

## 6. Neighbor Contagion

Nearby forms should not behave as independent islands.

When one motif destabilizes or reorganizes, nearby ones should slightly inherit:

- angle bias
- asymmetry
- fragmentation tendency
- brightness rhythm
- lobe dominance
- deformation direction

This should not become flocking or obvious synchronization.

It should feel more like **morphological contagion**.

### Goal
The field should feel coupled.

---

## 7. Soft Living Boundaries

Regions should not only exist logically.
Their boundaries should be active and fertile.

Near region boundaries:

- motifs should become more conflicted
- dialects should hybridize
- instability should increase
- impossible geometry should become more likely
- gradients may become ambiguous
- residue should thicken

This is where the field can become especially rich.

---

## 8. Era Shifts

On longer timescales, the field should slowly change emphasis.

Possible eras include:

- shell-heavy era
- scaffold-heavy era
- fracture-heavy era
- quiet-basin era
- pressure-front era
- manifold-weather era

These should drift gradually, not switch abruptly.

### Benefit
Longer viewing becomes rewarding.
The world feels less static and more alive over time.

---

## 9. Rare Near-Resolution Moments

Very occasionally, a local structure or cluster should almost become startlingly coherent.

Then it should:

- drift
- fail
- split
- collapse
- reinterpret

Because the field mostly resists resolution, a brief near-resolution moment becomes emotionally powerful.

### Rule
Use this rarely.
Its power comes from contrast.

---

## 10. Impossible Geometry via Overlap

This is promising, but it must remain emergent.

Do not draw explicit impossible objects.

Instead, use:

- contradictory depth cues
- partial contour sharing across layers
- overlaps that imply incompatible spatial readings
- shell fragments that belong to multiple depth readings
- manifold traces that seem both inside and outside nearby structures
- residue that disagrees with current structure

The result should feel:

- Escher-esque
- spatially unstable
- impossible but not illustrated

### Rule
Impossible geometry should arise as a consequence of crossings, residue, and ambiguity—not as a literal motif family.

---

# The Full Combined Doctrine

Claude should treat the system as having these laws:

## Law 1 — Climate over composition
The image is not arranged. It is weathered into temporary visibility.

## Law 2 — Beyond-frame continuity
Major structures belong to a world larger than the viewport.

## Law 3 — No stable symbols
Motifs must keep renegotiating identity.

## Law 4 — Gradients are evidence
Color and intensity variation should reveal climate and memory, not decorate geometry.

## Law 5 — Overlap creates contradiction
Spatial instability should emerge from crossings, residue, and incompatible depth cues.

## Law 6 — Memory matters
Structures carry the imprint of where they have been.

## Law 7 — Contagion matters
Nearby motifs should influence one another.

## Law 8 — Boundaries matter
The edges between climates are fertile, unstable, and visually rich.

## Law 9 — Time matters
The system should have eras, shocks, recovery, and weathering.

## Law 10 — Resolution is rare
Near-resolution may happen, but completion must fail gracefully.

# Recommended Implementation Order for Claude

Claude should not build this all at once as a blur. It should be built in layers.

## Phase 1 — Structural Engine Cleanup
- finish primitive ontology refactor
- remove circle-native assumptions
- refactor families and presets around shell / scaffold / spine / manifold / fragment logic
- keep debug overlays strong

## Phase 2 — Climate Engine
- implement dynamic multi-timescale field evolution
- strengthen regional dialects
- add living boundaries
- add era / seasonal drift

## Phase 3 — Identity and Memory
- strengthen identity half-life
- add motif memory
- add residue / scars
- add neighbor contagion
- add rare near-resolution moments

## Phase 4 — Macro and Off-Screen Continuity
- bias macro structures to start and end off-screen
- allow many mid-scale structures to be partially clipped
- redesign macro traces as weather, not rails
- strengthen macro-to-micro climate causality

## Phase 5 — Gradients and Impossible Geometry
- add climate-driven gradient system
- make gradients reflect pressure, memory, front direction, and decay
- introduce contradictory depth logic in overlap zones
- add impossible-geometry behavior as an emergent consequence, not an explicit motif type

## Phase 6 — Tuning
- reduce anything too literal
- reduce anything too decorative
- reduce anything too stable
- protect elegance
- keep the field readable enough to feel intentional
